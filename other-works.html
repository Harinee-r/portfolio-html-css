Doodles and Sketches

Doodling and sketching are always my first steps in any design process. They allow me to translate fleeting thoughts into visuals quickly, helping me capture ideas before they slip away.

The freedom that comes with a blank page and a pencil lets me explore concepts without constraints, testing shapes, forms, and layouts without overthinking. This spontaneous approach also sparks creativity and uncovers possibilities I hadn’t initially considered. Sketching serves as a foundation, giving me the freedom to refine and evolve my ideas into something more tangible and structured as the design takes shape. It’s where my imagination meets practicality, and the journey from concept to creation begins.

Vintage Playing Cards

Baroque art is known for its intricate details, drama, and bold contrasts. This style emphasizes grandeur, often seen in architecture and painting. I designed a set of four playing ace cards, coffee-dipped to achieve a vintage aesthetic. Each card is hand-drawn, reflecting the rich, detailed patterns of the Baroque era. Despite the time it took to recreate the same pattern on all four cards, this uniformity highlights the human touch behind the work.


Zine inspired by Japanese art

Japanese art is characterized by its simplicity, natural themes, and use of space. From delicate ink paintings to vibrant ukiyo-e prints, it focuses on minimalism and harmony with nature, making it timeless and distinct. A zine is a small, self-published booklet, often used to express personal stories, ideas, or art in a raw, creative format. In this zine, I’ve portrayed a snippet of my own life, blending humor with personal reflection. It offers a comical take on my daily experiences, all brought to life through Japanese-inspired visuals.


Poster and Book cover Design

This project uses the popular cartoon characters Phineas and Ferb to make learning about the digestive system fun and engaging for fifth graders. The poster was created using Adobe Illustrator, blending educational content with playful illustrations to keep students interested. For the book cover, I crafted a handmade mock-up using thermocol and cardboard, bringing the design to life with hand-drawn details using markers.

Stippling Art

Stippling is an art technique where tiny dots are used to create shading, texture, and form, giving depth to the subject. It requires patience and precision, as each dot contributes to the overall image. In this artwork, I portrayed a mystical creature, an elegant deer with long, graceful wings. The intricate stippling technique enhances the creature's ethereal nature, adding texture and life to the fantastical design.
